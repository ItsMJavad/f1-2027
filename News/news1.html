Desert Dominance: Javad Conquers Bahrain for Ferrari's Fourth Win of 2027

Bahrain International Circuit, Sakhir - Under the floodlights of the desert, Ferrari's Javad delivered another breathtaking performance to take his fourth victory of the 2027 Formula One season, extending his championship lead and solidifying Ferrari's position at the top of the standings.

Starting from fourth on the grid, the championship leader wasted no time making his mark. As the lights went out, Javad launched off the line with precision, threading through Hamilton and Verstappen before pulling off a daring move on Leclerc into Turn 4. By the end of the first lap, the red number 7 Ferrari was already in command — a lead he would never relinquish.

Behind him, Leclerc held steady in second place, unable to match Javad's pace but ensuring another Ferrari 1-2 finish, much to the delight of the Scuderia faithful. “He was flying today,” Leclerc admitted after the race. “Once he passed me, there was no catching him.”

Oscar Piastri capped off a strong weekend for McLaren by finishing third, his second consecutive podium and a valuable haul of points that keeps him firmly in the championship fight.

But the night was far from smooth for everyone. The Mercedes camp endured a nightmare race as both drivers retired early. Hamilton's race ended on lap 5 after his wheels locked under braking into Turn 10, spinning him into the gravel. Just two laps later, George Russell lost control in a tense battle between the two Saubers, bringing out a brief Virtual Safety Car and leaving the Silver Arrows empty-handed.

Nico Hülkenberg starred for Sauber, producing a stunning drive to fourth place — his best result of the season. The German's consistency and race craft earned him well-deserved praise in the paddock. Behind him, Verstappen's Red Bull suffered a mechanical issue that forced an unscheduled stop on lap 8, costing him two positions and leaving him frustrated in fifth.

Gabriel Bortoleto continued his impressive rookie campaign with another points finish in sixth, while Lando Norris recovered from early contact to bring his McLaren home seventh. Yuki Tsunoda took eighth for Red Bull after a quiet but composed race.

As the fireworks lit up the Bahraini sky, Javad's name once again echoed through the desert. With four wins in five races, the Ferrari driver looks untouchable — his combination of speed, precision, and race management turning him into the man to beat in 2027.

Ferrari now leads the Constructors' Championship comfortably, while Javad stretches his advantage to 42 points over teammate Leclerc. With momentum fully on his side, the question heading into Round 6 isn't who can stop Javad — but whether anyone can at all.
