Las Vegas Lights Up as Javad Makes It Five Wins in 2027

Las Vegas Strip Circuit - Round 6 of the 2027 Formula One World Championship

Under the neon glow of the Las Vegas Strip, Ferrari's unstoppable ace Javad claimed yet another flawless victory, extending his championship lead and continuing one of the most dominant season openings in modern F1 history.

Starting from pole after a commanding qualifying performance, Javad never looked back — not even once. As the field thundered into Turn 1, the Ferrari shot ahead cleanly, and from that moment on, he led every single lap, delivering a calm and calculated drive on a night where chaos struck nearly everyone except the championship leader.

A Night of Surges, Spins, and Heartbreak

Qualifying had already set the tone for drama. Max Verstappen suffered a heavy crash in Q2, forcing him to start P10, but the Dutchman bounced back strongly in the race, climbing up to a remarkable P4 by the flag.

McLaren had mixed fortunes. Lando Norris delivered one of the strongest drives of his season, holding a firm second place from early laps all the way to the finish. His teammate, Oscar Piastri, wasn't as fortunate — a spin on lap 9 dropped him to the back, and while he managed to rejoin, he could only recover to P9.

But the biggest heartbreak of the night belonged to Lewis Hamilton. The Mercedes driver held an excellent second place for most of the race after a strong qualifying, but with just ten laps remaining, a sudden mechanical issue saw his pace evaporate. Within minutes he tumbled down the order, slipping from P2 all the way to P5, salvaging points but losing a podium that had seemed certain.

George Russell finished just behind him in sixth, making the most of Mercedes' otherwise turbulent night.

Further back, Sauber delivered another consistent showing. Hülkenberg grabbed P7, with teammate Bortoleto close behind in P8, continuing the team's quiet but steady rise in the standings.
